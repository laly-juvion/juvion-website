The FlyGym Platform

High-throughput motor function screening using Drosophila melanogaster — the most genetically validated model for human aging.

What is FlyGym?

FlyGym is Juvion's proprietary robotics and AI platform for high-throughput screening of compounds that affect motor function. It uses Drosophila melanogaster — the common fruit fly — as a validated, cost-effective model for human neuromuscular aging.

The fruit fly shares 75% of human disease genes, enabling rapid compound discovery at a scale impossible with vertebrate models. Our platform quantifies motor function across four key dimensions simultaneously.

FlyGym combines computer vision, robotics, and machine learning to automatically track, score, and analyse thousands of flies across experimental conditions — generating publication-quality data in days rather than months.

Four Measurement Dimensions

FlyGym simultaneously quantifies motor performance across four key biological dimensions relevant to human healthy aging.

Toxicity
Compound safety screening at scale. Rapid identification of toxic compounds before progressing to further stages, dramatically reducing R&D risk.
Sleep
Circadian rhythm and rest quality quantification. Sleep disruption is a hallmark of aging — FlyGym tracks activity cycles with millisecond precision.
Mobility
Locomotion and movement pattern analysis. Automated tracking of gait, speed, and endurance — directly mapping to human motor aging endpoints.
Grip Strength
Neuromuscular function quantification. Grip strength decline is the most predictive biomarker of overall aging — measured across large populations simultaneously.

Why Drosophila?

75% Gene Homology

75% of human disease genes have a functional counterpart in Drosophila. The neuromuscular junction is structurally and functionally conserved between flies and humans.

Rapid Life Cycle

A full lifespan experiment completes in weeks, not years. This allows longitudinal aging studies and compound validation at a pace vertebrate models cannot match.

Cost-Effective Scale

Thousands of flies can be screened simultaneously at a fraction of the cost of mammalian studies — enabling statistically robust compound libraries to be tested economically.
