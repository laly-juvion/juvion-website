FlyGym Platform

High-throughput motor function screening using Drosophila melanogaster — the most genetically validated model for human aging.

What is FlyGym?

FlyGym is Juvion's proprietary robotics and AI platform for high-throughput screening of compounds that affect motor function. It uses Drosophila melanogaster — the common fruit fly — as a validated, cost-effective model for human neuromuscular aging.

The fruit fly shares 75% of human disease genes, enabling rapid compound discovery at a scale impossible with vertebrate models. Our platform quantifies motor function across three key dimensions simultaneously.

FlyGym combines computer vision, robotics, and machine learning to automatically track, score, and analyse thousands of flies across experimental conditions — generating publication-quality data in days rather than months.

Why Drosophila?

75% Gene Homology

75% of human disease genes have a functional counterpart in Drosophila. The neuromuscular junction is structurally and functionally conserved between flies and humans.

Rapid Life Cycle

A full lifespan experiment completes in weeks, not years. This allows longitudinal aging studies and compound validation at a pace vertebrate models cannot match.

Cost-Effective Scale

Thousands of flies can be screened simultaneously at a fraction of the cost of mammalian studies — enabling statistically robust compound libraries to be tested economically.

What FlyGym Measures

FlyGym simultaneously quantifies motor performance across key biological dimensions directly relevant to human healthy aging.

Toxicity & Lifespan

Rapid identification of toxic compounds and lifespan effects at scale. Screen thousands of compounds for safety and longevity impact before advancing to costly vertebrate studies — dramatically reducing R&D risk and timeline.

Mobility & Grip Strength

Automated locomotion tracking and neuromuscular function quantification. Gait, speed, endurance, and grip strength decline are the most predictive biomarkers of aging — measured simultaneously across large fly populations.

Humanized Drosophila in vivo

Disease-relevant fly models expressing human genes enable direct translational studies. Test compounds in a genetically humanized context that faithfully recapitulates human neuromuscular pathways and aging phenotypes.
